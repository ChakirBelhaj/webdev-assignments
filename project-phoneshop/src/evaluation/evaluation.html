Introduction

While Evaluating whether our page meets the requirements for success criteria level A to to the W3C Web Content Accessibility Guidelines. We were rather shocked how inaccessable our weppage was. Luckily we did some things atleast right. The page is for example not really accessible Visually, the website is insufficient for visually impaired people.

To regular users the usability of our webpage is positive.

For example:

  • The user can easily navigate through the tables to find information.
  • While not being disturbed by the background.
  • Possible actions are shown through right visual color presentation.
  • After an action is called, the user gets a message, wether the action was succesfull or not.
All in all, there is enough room for improvement, which we will point out in our improvement plan.

Accessibility highlights

1.3.2 Meaningful Sequence:

In our opinion this is an essential requirement for our webpage, the order of items presented on our webpage should make sense, this will prevent users from getting lost on our webpage. This Success Criterion may help people who rely on assistive technologies that read content aloud. The meaning evident in the sequencing of the information in the default presentation will be the same when the content is presented in spoken form.

2.4.3 Focus Order:

When tabbing through the application, the order must be in the right order. Sometimes a small portion of the page may be visible to an individual. Such a user may interpret a field in the wrong context if the focus order is not logical. This will cause frustration amongst users.

Appendix A

W3C Guideline Complies In our Page
Guideline 1.1 Text Alternatives: Provide text alternatives for any non-text content so that it can be changed into other forms people need, such as large print, braille, speech, symbols or simpler language. No
  • 1.1.1 Non-text Content: Our page fails to comply to this guideline, this because of the fact that we do not use any text alternatives in our webpage.
Guideline 1.3 Adaptable: Create content that can be presented in different ways (for example simpler layout) without losing information or structure. Yes
  • 1.3.1 Info and Relationships: Our information is displayed correctly.
  • 1.3.2 Meaningful Sequence: Our content on the page is presented in the right order, this will leave no questionmarks.
  • 1.3.3 Sensory Characteristics: Our content, for example buttons have the right color to identify their action.
Guideline 1.4 Distinguishable: Make it easier for users to see and hear content including separating foreground from background. No
  • 1.4.1 Use of Color: Color is not used as the only visual means of conveying information.
  • 1.4.2 Audio Control: There is no audio on our page, therefore no need for any audio control.
  • 1.4.3 Contrast (Minimum):the contrast ration on the page is not right.
  • 1.4.4 Resize text: Our text and images can be resized without assistive technology, this because of the fact that our webpage is responsive.
  • 1.4.5 Images of Text: Our visual presentation of text follows this principle.
  • 1.4.6 Contrast (Enhanced): Our webpage lacks the visual presentation contrast requirement.
  • 1.4.7 Low or No Background Audio: our page does not contain any audio.
  • 1.4.8 Visual Presentation: The visual presentation of block do possess the requirements, for example our width is no more than 80 characters or glyphs
  • 1.4.9 Images of Text (No Exception): We do not display any images of text on our webpage.
Guideline 2.1 Keyboard Accessible: Make all functionality available from a keyboard. Yes
  • 2.1.1 Keyboard: You can reach every page of the site and also use every function with the keyboard. The tab-function is the key.
  • 2.1.2 No Keyboard Trap: Our site does not contain a keyboard trap, you can switch focus anytime/anywhere you want with the keyboard. This is needed for people who rely on a keyboard including people who are f.e. blind and people with physical disabilities.
  • 2.1.3 Keyboard (No Exception): No timing needed for individual keystrokes.
Guideline 2.4 Navigable: Provide ways to help users navigate, find content, and determine where they are. No
  • 2.4.1 Bypass Blocks: Currently, there is no mechanism available to bypass blocks of contant that are repeated on multiple pages.
  • 2.4.2 Page Titled: The webpage currently has clear titles to describe the content, for example the table title with phones.
  • 2.4.3 Focus Order: Our buttons have a possess a color change on hover, this puts the focus on the button and is more userfriendly.
  • 2.4.4 Link Purpose (In Context): Our links on the page have a clear title, for example a link to our about page is called "about".
  • 2.4.5 Multiple Ways: We possess a navigation bar that makes navigation through the webpage userfriendly.
  • 2.4.6 Headings and Labels: Our phonestable is labeled accordingly.
  • 2.4.7 Focus Visible: Keyboard focus indicator is visible when tabbing through the webpage.
  • 2.4.8 Location: The navigation panel indicates where the user is at.
  • 2.4.9 Link Purpose (Link Only): Each link can be identified from link text alone.#download-section.
  • 2.4.10 Section Headings: Our page consists of multiple section headings, for example the table section headings.
